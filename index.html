MOOSE
A moose is a large herbivorous mammal native to North America, Europe, and Asia. Characterized by its long legs, broad shoulders, and a distinctive set of broad, palmate antlers (in males), it inhabits forests and wetlands. Moose are solitary, primarily feed on vegetation, and are excellent swimmers.

ELEPHANT
An elephant is a large, intelligent mammal known for its distinctive trunk, tusks, and large ears. Native to Africa and Asia, elephants are herbivores, living in complex social structures. They are highly empathetic, exhibit problem-solving abilities, and are crucial to ecosystems, maintaining forests and savannas by dispersing seeds.

POLAR BEAR
The polar bear is a large carnivorous mammal native to the Arctic region. Adapted to cold climates, it has a thick layer of fat and dense fur. Known for its white coat, it primarily hunts seals and is an excellent swimmer. Polar bears are threatened by climate change.

TIGER
The tiger is a large, powerful carnivorous cat native to Asia. Known for its striking orange coat with black stripes, it is a solitary predator, primarily hunting large mammals like deer and wild boar. Tigers are endangered due to habitat loss and poaching, with only a few thousand remaining in the wild.
